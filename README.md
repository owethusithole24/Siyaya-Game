# Siyaya-Game
A spin on the Age of Wheels: South Africa board game but with a little more South African flavour.

The Rules are simple:

There are two players, one blue and the other red. We'll leave it up to you to decide who is which but beware what you choose!
The blue player represents a Taxi whilst the Red player represents a bus.
The goal is to travel all around the map, collecting as much money as possible through each city and town entered; whoever gets to R200 first WINS!
But wait, it's not that simple. You need to be strategic about your travel routes as each route comes with its benefits and limitations.
Each player starts with a base amount of R120 and with each round you either win some or you lose some.
Players earn cash (points) at each town traveled; however, each major city deducts a hefty toll gate price.
Players can choose to invest in routes of their choosing at each round so that they directly affect the other players' choice of movement. 
If the opponent can wishes to pass through a route invested in by the other player they have to pay the owner a standard price to use it.
And get this! Some routes have police stationed between them; this creates a dynamic wherein players have to choose between paying the officers off (bribe) or forfeiting the next round.

Let the games begin!
